Utah just let an AI chatbot prescribe psychiatric medications without a doctor. Here's what that actually means.

By Plainly Staff  ·  April 5, 2026  ·  8 min read

For $19 a month, a chatbot in Utah will now renew your psychiatric medication — no doctor required. The state approved Legion Health, a Y Combinator-backed startup, to handle prescription renewals for 15 lower-risk psychiatric drugs including Prozac, Zoloft, and Lexapro. It's the second time a U.S. state has handed clinical authority to an algorithm, and it's launching right now.

We break down exactly how the system works, what the guardrails actually are, why psychiatrists are alarmed, and what it signals about where AI in healthcare is headed — in every state, not just Utah.
